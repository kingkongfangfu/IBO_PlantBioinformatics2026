This repository contains the official learning materials for the Plant Computational Biology and Bioinformatics training program developed for Filipino high school students preparing for the International Biology Olympiad (IBO) 2026.

The modules were prepared by researchers from the Philippine Genome Center R&D Program for Agriculture, Livestock, Fisheries, and Forestry. They provide an introduction to genomics, bioinformatics, and computational biology through lectures, hands-on activities, and case studies.

M 01 Plant Genome Fundamentals
45 min lecture 20 min workshop
▼
Materials Open module materials
Core Content
Topics
  • Nuclear, chloroplast, and mitochondrial genome organization
  • Gene structure: exon, intron, UTR, promoter
  • Coding vs. non-coding DNA; C-value paradox
  • Endosymbiotic origin of chloroplasts (Margulis)
Plant Examples
  • Arabidopsis: ~135 Mb nuclear, 154 kb chloroplast
  • Maize: ~2.4 Gb nuclear (85% transposable elements)
  • rbcL (cpDNA) vs. RBCS1A (nuclear, transit peptide)
  • ~130 protein-coding genes in the chloroplast genome
Key Skill
  • Classify genes as chloroplast-origin vs. nuclear-origin
  • Lowercase gene name (e.g., rbcL) = plastid-encoded convention
  • Uppercase (e.g., RBCS) = nuclear-encoded convention
  • Detect transit peptides using ChloroP or TargetP
Workshop Task
  • Classify 10 photosynthesis genes as nuclear or chloroplast
  • Justify one ambiguous case (e.g., FtsZ — nuclear but plastid function)
  • Sketch a simplified chloroplast genome map with LSC/SSC/IR regions
M 02 ORFs & Gene Annotation
45 min lecture 30 min workshop
▼
Materials Open module materials
Core Content
Topics
  • Six reading frames (3 forward, 3 reverse complement)
  • Start codon ATG; stop codons TAA, TAG, TGA
  • ORF = longest uninterrupted run between ATG and stop
  • GT-AG rule for plant intron splice sites
  • Kozak context around ATG affects translation efficiency
Skills
  • Identify the longest ORF in all 6 frames
  • Predict effect of nonsense vs. frameshift mutation
  • Distinguish ORF / CDS / transcript / mRNA
  • Identify 5' UTR, CDS, and 3' UTR from a GenBank entry
Common Traps
  • Longest ORF ≠ always correct gene (context required)
  • ORF ≠ functional gene (may be non-translated)
  • A single base insertion shifts ALL downstream codons
  • Intron retention from mutated GT or AG splice sites
Workshop
  • Given 200 bp sequence — locate ORF in all 6 frames
  • Introduce a +1 frameshift and predict new protein
  • Identify the splice sites in a 3-exon gene model
M 03 BLAST & Functional Inference
60 min lecture 45 min workshop
▼
Materials Open module materials
Core Content
Topics
  • BLAST algorithm: word seeding → extension → scoring
  • E-value: expected hits by chance (lower = more significant)
  • Percent identity vs. percent similarity (conservative subs)
  • Query coverage: what fraction of your sequence is matched
  • Bit score: independent of database size; comparable across runs
IBO Ambiguity Scenarios
  • High identity + low coverage → domain hit only
  • Multiple top hits with similar E-values → gene family
  • Bacterial hit with weak E-value on a plant gene → likely noise
  • Paralog confusion: D1 vs D2 of PSII (32% identity, full coverage)
Interpretation Rules
  • E < 1e-10: statistically significant
  • E < 1e-30: likely functional homolog
  • E > 0.01: discard — chance hit
  • Always combine E-value + identity + coverage
Workshop
  • Analyze 3 BLAST result tables for an unknown plant protein
  • Determine ortholog vs. paralog from hit patterns
  • Write a 2-sentence functional annotation with confidence level
M 04 Genome Browser Basics
45 min lecture 45 min workshop
▼
Materials Open module materials
Core Content
Tools
  • IGV (Integrative Genomics Viewer) — local, BAM/VCF visualization
  • UCSC Genome Browser — web, extensive track database
  • Ensembl Plants — plant-specific annotation, synteny
  • Coordinate format: Chr1:12345-67890
Track Reading
  • Thick blocks = exons; thin connecting lines = introns
  • Arrowheads on intron lines = direction of transcription
  • RNA-seq coverage height = relative expression level
  • Gap in RNA-seq coverage within a gene = likely intron

M 05 Multiple Sequence Alignment (MSA)
45 min lecture 30 min workshop
▼
Materials Open module materials
Core Content
Topics
  • ClustalW, MUSCLE, MAFFT — differences in algorithm speed vs. accuracy
  • Conservation scoring: * (identical) : (conserved) . (semi-conserved)
  • Gaps represent insertions/deletions (indels) in evolution
  • Gap penalty: opening penalty >> extension penalty
  • Positional entropy as a quantitative conservation measure
IBO Emphasis: Noisy Alignments
  • Low-complexity regions (poly-A, disordered) should be masked
  • N-terminal signal peptides: often poorly conserved — this is normal
  • Hypervariable C-terminus of D1 protein encodes degradation signal
  • Biologically meaningful conservation ≠ highest-scoring region
M 06 Phylogenetics & Plant Evolution
60 min lecture 45 min workshop
▼
Materials Open module materials
Core Content
Topics
  • Rooted vs. unrooted trees; outgroup placement
  • Bootstrap values: resampling confidence (≥70 = acceptable)
  • Orthologs (speciation) vs. paralogs (duplication)
  • Polyploidy: hexaploid wheat (AABBDD) → 3 homeolog sets
  • C4 photosynthesis: evolved >60 times independently in angiosperms
Ambiguous Trees — IBO Focus
  • Bootstrap <50: topology unreliable — report as unresolved
  • Long branch attraction: fast-evolving lineages cluster artificially
  • Polytomy = uncertainty, not simultaneous divergence
  • Gene tree ≠ species tree (incomplete lineage sorting)

Core Training Philosophy Rationale
"The modern IBO rewards biological reasoning under imperfect data. Comfort with uncertainty is more valuable than memorization."
Reason, Don't Memorize

Each module trains inference from incomplete data. IBO questions reward students who can justify a probabilistic conclusion, not those who can only recite facts.

Ambiguity Is the Test

Every module includes deliberate noise — conflicting BLAST hits, low bootstrap values, expression data with insufficient replicates. Practicing with messy data is essential.

Computation → Biology

Bioinformatics tools generate hypotheses. This training emphasizes that a prediction must always be connected back to a biological validation strategy.

Integration Is the Skill

The final case study combines seven data types simultaneously — reflecting how IBO questions and real research both require cross-dataset synthesis.
